# Travel Logistics Brief

## What This Skill Does
Produces a structured travel logistics brief covering routes, transit times, accommodation considerations, and crew movement planning for a reporting or production trip.

## When To Use This Skill
- You are planning a field trip and need to map out movement between multiple locations
- You need to brief a fixer, production coordinator, or travel agent on crew travel requirements
- You are estimating travel days and costs for a budget or schedule
- You are traveling to an unfamiliar region and need to identify which parts of the journey require local expertise or advance booking
- You are coordinating movement for a crew of more than two people across multiple travel legs

## What You Need To Provide
**Required:**
- Origin city or country
- Destination(s) — can be multiple locations if the trip involves movement between sites
- Approximate travel dates or production window
- Crew size and roles

**Optional:**
- Equipment being carried (affects transport choices and border requirements)
- Budget sensitivity (economy vs. flexible fares vs. chartered transport)
- Any known constraints (visa restrictions, equipment import permits, remote areas without commercial transport)
- Specific concerns to address (health precautions, local fixers needed, satellite connectivity)

## How Claude Approaches This
1. Maps the journey from origin to each destination in logical sequence, identifying the most practical routing for a working crew — not just the fastest route for a solo traveller
2. Flags each leg for crew-specific concerns: equipment handling, transit times that affect shooting schedules, legs where commercial transport is unavailable or unreliable, and border crossings requiring advance preparation
3. Produces a brief organized as an actionable task list for a production coordinator, distinguishing between items that must be booked far in advance, items to arrange on arrival, and items requiring specialist advice (fixers, security consultants, medical)

## Output Format
A structured document of 500–800 words organized into: Journey Overview (legs listed in sequence with estimated transit times), Logistics Flags (anything that needs advance action), Accommodation Considerations, Equipment and Customs Notes, and Recommended Next Steps. Practical and direct — written for a production coordinator who needs to make bookings, not a travel blogger. Includes honest notes where local knowledge is essential and Claude's information may be out of date.

## Quality Criteria
- [ ] Every travel leg is described with estimated time and primary transport option
- [ ] Equipment carrying is addressed if crew is traveling with kit
- [ ] At least one "advance booking required" flag is identified where realistic
- [ ] Accommodation considerations are specific to the production context (not generic hotel recommendations)
- [ ] Document is usable as a briefing note to a fixer or travel agent without rewriting

## Example

### Input
Origin: London, UK
Destinations: Tbilisi, Georgia (2 days) → Mestia, Svaneti region (3 days) → back to Tbilisi (1 day) → London
Production window: Mid-October
Crew: Director, camera operator, sound recordist (3 people)
Equipment: Full documentary camera kit — 8 cases and bags, approx. 120kg total
Concerns: Remote mountain location, limited connectivity, uncertain road conditions in October

### Output

# Travel Logistics Brief — Georgia Documentary Trip
**Crew:** 3 (Director / Camera Operator / Sound Recordist)
**Route:** London → Tbilisi → Mestia (Svaneti) → Tbilisi → London
**Production window:** Mid-October
**Equipment load:** ~120kg / 8 cases and bags

---

## Journey Overview

### Leg 1 — London to Tbilisi
**Transport:** Direct flights operate London (LHR/LGW) to Tbilisi (TBS) — journey time approximately 5 hours. Multiple carriers operate this route; check baggage allowance policies carefully as excess baggage fees on this route can be significant for a full camera kit.
**Estimated travel day:** 1 full day (allow for check-in time with heavy equipment load)
**Key note:** Georgia has no specific carnets or temporary import permits required for professional camera equipment for documentary use, but customs declarations at Tbilisi airport should be made clearly on arrival. Brief your camera operator to carry a detailed equipment manifest.

### Leg 2 — Tbilisi to Mestia (Svaneti)
**Transport option A — Scheduled flight:** Vanilla Sky operates small aircraft on the Tbilisi–Mestia route (approx. 45 minutes). This is the preferred option given equipment load, but the aircraft is small (capacity ~18 seats) and cargo hold is very limited. Three crew plus 120kg of equipment will likely require two flights or a separate cargo arrangement — confirm directly with the airline.
**Transport option B — Road:** Tbilisi to Mestia by road is approximately 430km, typically 7–9 hours in good conditions. In mid-October, the Zemo Svaneti road may be affected by early snowfall above 2,000m — road conditions are unreliable. A 4×4 vehicle (or two vehicles for crew + equipment) is essential; a minivan is not adequate.
**Recommended approach:** Fly crew on the scheduled flight; arrange a separate vehicle with local driver to transport equipment by road the same day, meeting in Mestia. This splits risk and avoids equipment hold limitations.
**Estimated travel time:** Half day (flight option) or full day (road option)

### Leg 3 — Mestia local movement
**Transport:** Mestia town is small; filming locations in Svaneti typically require 4×4 transport on unpaved mountain tracks. A local vehicle with driver is essential for the duration. Arrange through your Tbilisi fixer in advance — do not rely on finding suitable transport in Mestia on arrival.
**Key note:** In mid-October, some high-altitude locations in Svaneti may already be inaccessible due to early snow. Confirm specific location access with your fixer at least two weeks before travel.

### Leg 4 — Mestia to Tbilisi
**Transport:** Same options as Leg 2 in reverse. If the scheduled flight is available, use it. Allow a half-day buffer before any onward international flight from Tbilisi — the Mestia road is prone to delays.

### Leg 5 — Tbilisi to London
Same routing as Leg 1. Allow time for equipment re-check and customs clearance on departure.

---

## Logistics Flags

**Book in advance:**
- Flights London–Tbilisi (excess baggage must be pre-arranged with airline, not paid at check-in for this volume)
- Vanilla Sky Tbilisi–Mestia flights — small capacity, book as early as possible for mid-October
- Local 4×4 vehicles in Mestia for the full 3-day production period (limited availability)
- Tbilisi-based fixer with experience in Svaneti — essential for equipment transport coordination, accommodation, and location access

**Check before travel:**
- Road condition reports for the Zemo Svaneti highway (Georgian Road Department updates)
- Mestia airport status — the strip occasionally closes due to weather; have a road plan as backup
- Health: Georgia requires no specific vaccinations for standard travel, but confirm current requirements and carry comprehensive travel insurance including medical evacuation

---

## Accommodation Considerations
Mestia has a small but functional range of guesthouses suitable for a three-person crew. Booking through your fixer is strongly recommended over online platforms — local operators are more reliable and can accommodate early morning call times and equipment storage needs. In mid-October, tourism season is winding down; availability should not be a problem but some guesthouses close for winter — confirm operating dates.

In Tbilisi, standard hotels in the city centre are adequate for crew; choose a hotel with secure storage or a luggage room for equipment cases during your Svaneti leg if leaving kit behind in Tbilisi is preferable to transporting everything.

## Equipment and Customs Notes
- Carry a printed, itemized equipment manifest (in English; Georgian translation helpful) for all border crossings and customs interactions
- Equipment declared on arrival in Tbilisi should be declared again on departure — retain all customs paperwork throughout the trip
- Batteries (lithium): airline restrictions apply to the number and capacity of lithium batteries in checked luggage; confirm current rules with your carrier for the specific battery types you are carrying
- Equipment transported by road should be packed to withstand rough mountain tracks — padded hard cases are essential; soft bags should go in the passenger compartment, not the boot

## Recommended Next Steps
1. Engage a Tbilisi-based fixer with Svaneti experience — this is the highest-priority action and affects all other logistics
2. Book London–Tbilisi flights with excess baggage pre-arranged
3. Contact Vanilla Sky directly regarding cargo capacity for the Mestia flight
4. Fixer to arrange Mestia accommodation and local vehicles
5. Check road conditions and Mestia strip status two weeks before departure and again 48 hours before the Svaneti leg

## Known Limitations
- Transport schedules, road conditions, and airline policies change frequently; all specific details (flight routes, carrier names, transit times) must be verified directly at the time of planning — Claude's knowledge has a cutoff date and cannot reflect current conditions
- Claude cannot assess security conditions, political situations, or health advisories — consult your country's foreign travel advisory service before travel to any location flagged as sensitive
- Local fixer quality varies significantly; Claude cannot recommend specific individuals or agencies — ask for references from other production companies who have worked in the region
