# Broadcaster Pitch Writer

## What This Skill Does
Writes a structured broadcaster pitch document for a documentary or series, covering logline, target audience, editorial angle, comparable titles, production approach, and financing status.

## When To Use This Skill
- You are preparing to pitch a documentary project to a commissioning editor at a broadcaster or streaming platform
- You need a written document to leave behind after a verbal pitch meeting
- You are approaching multiple broadcasters and need a consistent, professional pitch document to adapt per recipient
- You have a treatment or series bible but need a shorter, sharper pitch-format document for initial outreach

## What You Need To Provide
**Required:**
- Working title of the project
- One-paragraph description of the film or series (what it's about, who it follows, what happens)
- Target broadcaster or platform (even approximate: "factual cable channel," "public broadcaster," "SVOD platform")
- Format: single film, limited series, or ongoing series — and runtime per episode/film
- Your production company name (or "independent producer")

**Optional:**
- Two or three comparable titles (films or series that are similar in tone, subject, or audience)
- Access or talent already attached (key interview subjects, archive, expert contributors)
- Production status (development, pre-production, in production)
- Budget range or financing already in place
- Any awards, development support, or prior broadcaster interest already received

## How Claude Approaches This
1. Opens with a logline and a brief editorial hook — the one reason this story matters right now — tailored to the stated broadcaster's brand and audience
2. Builds out the pitch body: subject and story, characters or contributors, narrative structure, visual approach, and why this producer/team is the right one to make it
3. Adds a comps section that positions the project accurately in the market — comps are chosen to signal tone and ambition, not just genre
4. Closes with a production and financing status block, and a clear call to action
5. Trims the entire document to fit on two pages or fewer, cutting any language that sounds like a treatment rather than a sales pitch

## Output Format
- Length: 400–600 words (two pages at standard business formatting)
- Structure: Logline → Editorial Hook → The Story → Characters/Contributors → Visual & Narrative Approach → Comparable Titles → Production Status → Contact/Call to Action
- Tone: confident, specific, editorial — not promotional or hyperbolic
- No bullet points in the pitch body (prose paragraphs only); bullet points acceptable only in the Production Status block
- Written in third person (the project is described as "the film" or "the series")

## Quality Criteria
- [ ] The logline is one sentence that contains the subject, the angle, and a sense of stakes
- [ ] The editorial hook explains why this story is urgent or timely — not just interesting
- [ ] Comparable titles are real, recent, and accurately positioned (not aspirational overclaims)
- [ ] The pitch does not describe scenes or sequences in detail — it sells the concept and the team
- [ ] The closing block states production status, budget range (if provided), and a specific next step

## Example

### Input
- **Title:** The Last Frequency
- **Description:** A film about the final generation of shortwave radio operators across Europe — elderly men and women who built clandestine radio networks during the Cold War and now maintain their stations in near-total obscurity. The film follows three of them over one year as they face the shutdown of the last public shortwave broadcast infrastructure.
- **Target broadcaster:** European public broadcaster (factual/documentary strand)
- **Format:** Single film, 75 minutes
- **Production company:** Northlight Documentary
- **Comps:** "The Cave" (National Geographic, 2019), "Into the Ice" (Netflix, 2022)
- **Status:** Development. Director attached. Two of three subjects confirmed. No financing in place.

### Output

**THE LAST FREQUENCY**
*A documentary film · 75 minutes · Northlight Documentary*

---

**Logline**
As Europe dismantles its last shortwave broadcast infrastructure, three former Cold War radio operators face the erasure of a network they built in secret — and the silence that follows.

**Why Now**
In 2024, the final state-operated shortwave transmitter networks in Western Europe are scheduled for decommission. When they go dark, so does the living memory of a communication system that once reached behind the Iron Curtain without a single satellite. *The Last Frequency* arrives at exactly the moment when that history becomes irretrievable.

**The Story**
Across three countries, three people maintain shortwave stations that the world has forgotten. There is a retired engineer in the Netherlands who still broadcasts weather data to ships that no longer listen. A former resistance radio operator in Poland who transmits every Sunday morning out of habit she cannot name. And an Austrian archivist who has spent twenty years recording signals no living person intentionally sends. Over twelve months, *The Last Frequency* follows these three figures as the infrastructure around them is switched off, their equipment declared obsolete, and the bureaucratic process of erasure accelerates.

The film is not about nostalgia. It is about what it costs to maintain a form of knowledge that society has decided it no longer needs — and about the particular stubbornness of people who refuse to stop transmitting.

**Approach**
The visual language is observational and precise: the interior architecture of analogue equipment, the quality of light in rooms where time has stopped, the physical act of listening. No reconstruction, no archive footage. The film earns its emotional weight entirely from the present tense.

**Comparable Titles**
*The Cave* (National Geographic Documentary Films, 2019) — for its immersive, character-led approach to a world most viewers have never seen. *Into the Ice* (Netflix, 2022) — for the combination of specialist expertise and personal stakes in a film addressed to a broad international audience.

**Production Status**
- Stage: Development
- Director: [attached — available on request]
- Subjects: Two of three confirmed; third in negotiation
- Financing: Seeking first broadcast partner as lead commissioning editor
- Budget range: €600K–€800K (estimated; formal budget on request)

We are seeking a development conversation with [broadcaster] as a potential lead partner. We are happy to share the full treatment, director's statement, and subject access letters at your request.

**Contact:** Northlight Documentary · development@northlightdoc.com

## Known Limitations
- Claude cannot verify whether comparable titles are accurate market comps — the user must confirm that stated comps are genuinely similar in audience, budget tier, and tone, not merely in subject matter
- The pitch document produced here is a starting-point draft; experienced commissioners will identify generic language that needs personalizing to their specific strand or editorial identity
- Claude does not know the current commissioning priorities of specific broadcasters; statements like "this fits [broadcaster]'s strand" should be verified by the user before submission
- For series formats, a single pitch document may be insufficient — most commissioners expect a full series bible at some stage of the conversation
