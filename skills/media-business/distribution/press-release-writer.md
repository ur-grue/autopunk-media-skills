# Press Release Writer

## What This Skill Does
Writes a professional press release announcing a premiere, commission, award win, or production milestone for a media project, formatted for distribution to entertainment journalists and trade press.

## When To Use This Skill
- A project has been commissioned, greenlit, or picked up and needs a formal announcement
- A film or series is premiering at a festival and you need press materials ready on announcement day
- A project has won an award or been selected for a significant programme
- A production company is announcing a major new partnership, co-production, or distribution deal
- A podcast or YouTube channel is launching a new season or hitting a milestone

## What You Need To Provide
**Required:** The announcement being made (what exactly happened), project title and format, production company name, key talent involved (director, presenter, executive producer), and the outlet or distributor involved if applicable.

**Optional:** Broadcast or release date, quotes from key talent or executives, brief project description or synopsis, any other context (awards history, previous series, production background), embargo date if applicable.

## How Claude Approaches This
1. Writes a headline that states the news factually — who did what — without promotional language or vague teasers
2. Opens the body with a lead paragraph that delivers the essential news (who, what, when, where) in 40–60 words, then expands with project description, talent quotes, and background context in descending order of news value
3. Closes with a standard Notes to Editors section covering production company background, contact details placeholder, and any relevant technical details (running time, broadcaster, release window)

## Output Format
Headline + body copy + Notes to Editors. Body: 350–500 words. Standard AP/UK trade press format: double-spaced paragraphs, quotes in their own paragraphs, boilerplate at the end. Professional and factual throughout — no marketing language, no exclamation marks, no claims the journalist cannot verify. All quotes clearly attributed. Embargo line at the top if applicable.

## Quality Criteria
- [ ] Headline states the news in under 12 words, no superlatives
- [ ] Lead paragraph answers who, what, when, and where without requiring the reader to continue
- [ ] At least one talent quote included and clearly attributed
- [ ] Project description is factual, not promotional
- [ ] Notes to Editors section present with production company background and contact placeholder
- [ ] No marketing language ("groundbreaking," "must-see," "landmark") anywhere in the copy

## Known Limitations
- Talent quotes must be supplied by the user — the skill will generate placeholder quotes if none are provided, but these must be replaced with real approved quotes before distribution. Never send a press release with fabricated quotes.
- The skill formats for UK and European trade press conventions. US trade press (Deadline, Variety) has slightly different style conventions (all-caps titles, specific formatting) that may require minor manual adjustment.
- Press releases for streaming platforms may need to follow the platform's own PR template requirements, which vary by distributor and cannot be anticipated here.
