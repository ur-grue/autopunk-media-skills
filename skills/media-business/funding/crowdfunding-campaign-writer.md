# Crowdfunding Campaign Writer

## What This Skill Does
Writes the campaign description, reward tier copy, and campaign update text for a crowdfunding campaign for a media project on platforms like Kickstarter, Indiegogo, or Seed&Spark.

## When To Use This Skill
- You are launching a crowdfunding campaign for a documentary, podcast, or independent media project
- You have a campaign page that is not converting and want to rewrite the description
- You need to write reward tier descriptions that feel worth backing at each price point
- You need campaign update emails or posts to keep existing backers engaged during the campaign

## What You Need To Provide
**Required:** Project title and format, brief description of the project (what it is and why you are making it), fundraising goal, primary audience or community you are appealing to.

**Optional:** Your background or credentials as a filmmaker or producer, the specific platform (Kickstarter, Indiegogo, etc.), your planned reward tiers and the amounts, what the money will specifically be used for, any existing community (newsletter subscribers, social media following, previous project audience).

## How Claude Approaches This
1. Writes the campaign headline and opening paragraph to hook the specific community the project belongs to — not a general audience pitch but a direct address to the people who already care about this subject
2. Writes the campaign body copy covering: what the project is, why it matters now, who is making it, and exactly what the money will fund — each section in plain language that respects the backer's intelligence
3. Writes reward tier descriptions that justify each price point with something real (access, acknowledgement, physical objects, involvement) — and writes two sample campaign updates: one mid-campaign milestone update and one campaign-end thank-you

## Output Format
Three deliverables: (1) Campaign page copy — headline, opening hook paragraph, full description body (400–600 words), and three-sentence campaign summary for social sharing; (2) Reward tier descriptions — 2–4 tiers with suggested amounts and copy for each; (3) Two sample campaign update posts (150–200 words each). Conversational but purposeful register — the voice of someone making something they believe in, not a sales deck.

## Quality Criteria
- [ ] Opening paragraph addresses the project's core community or audience directly
- [ ] Campaign body answers: what it is, why now, who is making it, what the money does
- [ ] Reward tiers are specific — no vague "exclusive content" without describing what it is
- [ ] Each tier's value is proportionate to its price point
- [ ] Campaign updates have a specific milestone or piece of news — not generic "we're so grateful" copy
- [ ] No hyperbole ("revolutionary," "life-changing," "groundbreaking")

## Example

### Input
Project: Saltwater — a feature documentary following a marine biologist and a fishing community in conflict over protected ocean zones. The scientist wants them closed; the fishing families depend on them for income. Shot over three years.
Format: Feature documentary, 85 minutes
Goal: €35,000
Platform: Kickstarter
What money funds: Post-production — edit, colour grade, sound mix, festival submission fees
Audience: Ocean conservation community, documentary enthusiasts, people from fishing communities
My background: First-time feature director; my short film played at two international festivals
Reward tiers I'm thinking of: €10, €25, €75, €150

### Output

---

## Campaign Page Copy

**Headline:** SALTWATER: A documentary three years in the making — help us finish it

---

**Opening hook:**
If you've ever stood on a pier watching the last fishing boats come in, or followed the news on marine protected areas and wondered what the people actually living there think — this film is for you.

---

**Campaign description:**

SALTWATER is a feature documentary I've spent three years making on the Atlantic coast. It follows two people who are locked in one of the most common conflicts in ocean conservation: a marine biologist who has spent a decade lobbying for the closure of a protected fishing zone, and a family who has fished that zone for four generations and whose livelihood depends on it remaining open.

I've been filming both sides. I've been in the scientist's lab when the data comes in. I've been on the boat at five in the morning. I've been in the room when they had to sit across a table from each other.

The film doesn't take sides. It asks what it costs to be right.

**We have everything we need except the finishing money.**

Principal photography is complete. We have 180 hours of footage, a cut in progress, and the full cooperation of everyone in the film. What we need to finish SALTWATER is €35,000 for post-production: the edit, colour grade, sound mix, and the submission fees to get it to the festivals where people will actually see it.

Your backing pays for the specific, unsexy, essential work that turns footage into a film.

**About the director:**
This is my first feature documentary. My short film *Drift* (2022) played at festivals in four countries. SALTWATER is the bigger story I needed more time for.

---

**Three-sentence social summary:**
SALTWATER is a documentary about the real human cost of ocean conservation, three years in the making. We follow a marine biologist and a fishing family in direct conflict over a protected zone — and we let the complexity breathe. Help us finish it.

---

## Reward Tiers

**€10 — Early Supporter**
Your name in the credits. You helped make this happen, and we'll make sure that's on the record.

**€25 — Credit + Digital Film**
Your name in the credits, plus a digital copy of SALTWATER when it's released. Watch it. Show it to people.

**€75 — Credit + Digital Film + Director's Notes**
Everything above, plus a written director's commentary on five scenes — what you see, and what happened around it that didn't make the cut.

**€150 — Credit + Digital Film + Behind the Scenes Access**
Everything above, plus access to a private online Q&A with the director and subjects after the premiere. Ask the questions the film doesn't answer.

---

## Campaign Update 1 — Midpoint Milestone (Sample)

**Subject: 60% funded — and something we can finally share**

We're at 60% with 14 days to go, and we want to tell you something we've been holding back.

The scene we were most uncertain about — the one where the scientist and the fishing family's eldest son finally sit in the same room — we filmed it. We watched it back. It's in the film.

We can't share it yet. But we want you to know it exists, and that it changes what this film is.

If you've been sitting on the fence: this is the week to back it. Share the campaign link if you have five seconds. Every backer tells the algorithm the project is real.

Thank you for being here.

---

## Campaign Update 2 — Final 48 Hours (Sample)

**Subject: 48 hours left**

Two days.

We're at 78% and we need to close this. If we don't hit the goal, Kickstarter returns every pledge and we go back to looking for finishing money the slow way.

We'd rather finish the film.

If you've been meaning to back this: now is the time. If you've already backed it: share it with one person today. That's all we're asking.

SALTWATER is real. The people in it are real. The money goes directly to finishing what we started three years ago.

Thank you — genuinely.

## Known Limitations
- Crowdfunding campaign success depends heavily on the size and engagement of your existing community before launch. This skill can produce excellent copy, but copy alone will not fund a campaign without an audience to read it.
- Reward tier amounts are suggestions based on the user's input. The right tier amounts for a given community vary significantly; the user should research comparable campaigns in their niche before finalising.
- Campaign update copy requires real news (a milestone, a finished scene, a piece of press coverage). If there is no genuine news to share, the update will feel hollow regardless of how well it is written.
