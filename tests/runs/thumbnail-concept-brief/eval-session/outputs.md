# Thumbnail Concept Brief — Eval Outputs

## Input 01: Standard YouTube explainer
**Prompt:** Video title: Why You Can't Focus Anymore. Target audience: knowledge workers 25-40. Core emotion: Recognition — the viewer should feel seen, like someone finally named their problem. Channel style: Clean white backgrounds, host on right, bold text on left.

### Output

**Thumbnail Brief — "Why You Can't Focus Anymore"**

**One-Frame Promise:** The viewer should instantly recognize their own struggle — this thumbnail names what they have been feeling but could not articulate.

---

**Concept 1: The Overwhelmed Screen**
- *Emotional Hook:* Recognition — "that's me"
- *Background:* Clean white (#FFFFFF) with a subtle grid of faded app icons, browser tabs, and notification badges filling the left two-thirds — just visible enough to register as visual noise
- *Foreground/Subject:* Host on right side of frame, expression: exhausted but knowing — slight eye squint, hand on forehead, looking directly at camera
- *Text Overlay:* Left-aligned, two lines — "WHY YOU" (black, large, sans-serif) / "CAN'T FOCUS" (red #CC0000, slightly larger, bold). Clean and punchy.
- *Color Mood:* Predominantly white with red accent text — the simplicity itself communicates clarity amid chaos
- *Production Notes:* Shoot host against white backdrop. App icons in background can be created in Photoshop at 20% opacity. Host expression must feel genuine, not theatrical. This is the primary publish concept.

---

**Concept 2: The Split Brain**
- *Emotional Hook:* Curiosity — something is wrong and I want to understand it
- *Background:* White, split down the center with a thin red (#CC0000) vertical line
- *Foreground/Subject:* Host on right side, looking slightly off-center as if distracted. Left side: a simple illustration or icon of a brain with arrows pointing outward in multiple directions — fragmentation visual
- *Text Overlay:* Left-aligned above the brain graphic — "YOUR BRAIN" (black) / "IS BROKEN" (red #CC0000). Deliberately provocative.
- *Color Mood:* High contrast — white, black, red. Medical/diagnostic feel.
- *Production Notes:* The brain graphic should be clean and diagrammatic, not cartoonish. Can use a simple vector or Canva element. Keep total elements to three: text, brain, host. Test readability at mobile size.

---

**Concept 3: Text-Only (Mobile-First)**
- *Emotional Hook:* Direct confrontation — no face to soften the message
- *Background:* Solid white (#FFFFFF)
- *Foreground/Subject:* No host. Text only.
- *Text Overlay:* Three stacked lines, centered — "YOU" (black, medium) / "CAN'T" (red #CC0000, massive, dominant) / "FOCUS" (black, medium). The word "CAN'T" is at least 3x the size of the other words.
- *Color Mood:* Stark white and red — maximum contrast, reads clearly at any size including mobile thumbnail
- *Production Notes:* Use a heavy sans-serif font (e.g., Montserrat Black, Impact). Test at 120x68px to confirm the word "CAN'T" is legible. Recommended as A/B test on mobile against Concept 1.

**Recommended for primary publish:** Concept 1. A/B test Concept 3 for mobile performance.

**Next Step:** Open your design tool and build Concept 1 first. If your text overlay copy differs from your final SEO title, run seo-title-optimizer to align them.

## Input 02: Niche technical topic
**Prompt:** Video title: How LIDAR Changed Archaeology Forever. Target audience: science-curious viewers and archaeology enthusiasts. Core emotion: Wonder — the viewer should feel like a lost world is being revealed. No presenter face.

### Output

**Thumbnail Brief — "How LIDAR Changed Archaeology Forever"**

**One-Frame Promise:** The viewer should feel they are seeing something hidden — a world beneath the surface being revealed for the first time.

---

**Concept 1: The Reveal**
- *Emotional Hook:* Wonder and discovery — a hidden structure emerging from dense jungle
- *Background:* Aerial view of dense green jungle canopy (stock or generated), with the right half digitally stripped away to reveal a LIDAR point-cloud rendering of ancient ruins beneath — glowing cyan (#00CED1) and white lines on dark background
- *Foreground/Subject:* No presenter. The image is the subject — the before/after split of jungle vs. revealed structure
- *Text Overlay:* Bottom-left, two lines — "HIDDEN FOR" (white, medium) / "1,000 YEARS" (cyan #00CED1, large, bold). Period creates drama.
- *Color Mood:* Deep green and cyan — natural world meeting technology. The LIDAR side should glow against darkness.
- *Production Notes:* Use a real LIDAR visualization if available (many are public domain from archaeological surveys). If compositing, the split line between jungle and LIDAR should be rough/organic, not a clean geometric divide. Ensure the ruins are recognizable as structures at thumbnail size.

---

**Concept 2: The Data Landscape**
- *Emotional Hook:* Awe at scale — technology revealing an entire civilization
- *Background:* Full-frame LIDAR point cloud visualization — a sweeping landscape of structures, roads, and terrain in glowing green (#39FF14) on black (#000000)
- *Foreground/Subject:* No presenter. The LIDAR landscape fills the entire frame like a digital topographic map.
- *Text Overlay:* Top-left corner, clean — "LIDAR" (green #39FF14, large, monospace font) / "CHANGED EVERYTHING" (white, smaller, sans-serif)
- *Color Mood:* Black and neon green — feels like looking at classified satellite data. Technical, futuristic.
- *Production Notes:* Source LIDAR imagery from public archaeological datasets (e.g., the Angkor or Maya surveys). The landscape must show recognizable human-made structures — roads, platforms, buildings — not just terrain. Mobile-optimized: high contrast, minimal text, image does the work.

---

**Concept 3: The Comparison (Mobile-First)**
- *Emotional Hook:* Surprise — the same place looks completely different
- *Background:* Side-by-side split. Left: aerial photo of dense, featureless jungle. Right: the same area rendered in LIDAR showing a complex ancient city grid in electric blue (#1E90FF) lines on black.
- *Foreground/Subject:* No presenter. The two images are the entire concept.
- *Text Overlay:* Centered across the split line — "WHAT WE MISSED" (white, bold, large). Simple and immediate.
- *Color Mood:* Natural green vs. electric blue — the contrast between human vision and machine vision
- *Production Notes:* The before/after must be the same geographic area for the comparison to land. Label "PHOTO" and "LIDAR" in small text at the top of each half. Test at mobile thumbnail size — the structural difference between the two halves must be obvious even at 120x68px.

**Recommended for primary publish:** Concept 1 — the split-reveal is the most immediately striking. Test Concept 3 as mobile variant.

**Next Step:** Source LIDAR imagery from public datasets or generate a representative visualization. Brief your designer with Concept 1. Run seo-title-optimizer if you want to verify that the text overlay language aligns with your SEO title.

## Input 03: Minimal input
**Prompt:** Video title: Making Sourdough. Target audience: home bakers. Core emotion: Warmth.

### Output

**Thumbnail Brief — "Making Sourdough"**

**One-Frame Promise:** The viewer should feel the warmth of home baking — this looks inviting, achievable, and satisfying.

---

**Concept 1: The Golden Loaf**
- *Emotional Hook:* Warmth and satisfaction — the perfect result you want to achieve
- *Background:* Warm-toned wooden surface (rustic cutting board or farmhouse table), soft natural light from the left, slightly shallow depth of field
- *Foreground/Subject:* A freshly baked sourdough loaf, just scored and cracked open, with steam still visible. The crust is deep golden-brown. A linen cloth or flour-dusted surface adds texture.
- *Text Overlay:* Bottom-left, two lines — "MAKING" (cream #FFF8DC, medium) / "SOURDOUGH" (warm white, large, serif font). Understated and elegant.
- *Color Mood:* Warm amber (#D4A574) and cream tones — golden hour kitchen light. No cool tones anywhere.
- *Production Notes:* Shoot with natural light or warm artificial light (3200K). Avoid overhead fluorescent lighting. The loaf should look homemade, not bakery-perfect — slight imperfections make it feel achievable. If using stock, find an image that looks rustic, not commercial.

---

**Concept 2: The Hands-In-Dough**
- *Emotional Hook:* Tactile connection — the viewer wants to feel what this feels like
- *Background:* Flour-dusted dark surface (dark wood or slate, charcoal #36454F) — creates contrast with the pale dough
- *Foreground/Subject:* Hands shaping a round of sourdough dough, lightly floured. Close-up, cropped at the wrists. The dough has visible air bubbles.
- *Text Overlay:* Top-right corner — "SOURDOUGH" (warm white, large, bold) / "from scratch" (smaller, italic, cream). Positioned away from the hands.
- *Color Mood:* Dark background with warm foreground — the dough and hands glow against the dark surface. Cozy, intimate.
- *Production Notes:* Shoot top-down or at 45-degree angle. The flour dust should be visible and atmospheric, not messy. Hands should look clean and capable, not studio-model-perfect. Mobile-optimized: the contrast between light dough and dark surface reads well at small size.

---

**Concept 3: The Starter Close-Up (Mobile-First)**
- *Emotional Hook:* Curiosity — what is this bubbly, alive-looking thing?
- *Background:* Tight close-up, minimal context — just the rim of a glass jar
- *Foreground/Subject:* A sourdough starter at peak activity — bubbly, risen, visibly alive. Shot through glass so the bubble structure is visible. Warm light hitting the jar from one side.
- *Text Overlay:* Centered, overlaying the jar — "IT'S ALIVE" (warm white, bold, playful). Two words, maximum impact.
- *Color Mood:* Warm honey tones (#DAA520) — the starter itself provides the color palette. Background falls to soft blur.
- *Production Notes:* Shoot macro or close-up with a smartphone in good light. The bubbles must be clearly visible — this is the visual hook. Test at mobile size: the bubble texture and "IT'S ALIVE" text must both read clearly.

**Recommended for primary publish:** Concept 1 — the golden loaf is the most universally appealing to home bakers. Test Concept 3 if your audience skews toward curious beginners.

**Next Step:** Shoot the loaf photo in natural light. Open Canva or Photoshop and build Concept 1 with the warm color palette specified. Run seo-title-optimizer if you want more descriptive title options for the text overlay.

## Input 04: European/international context
**Prompt:** Video title: Inside Barcelona's Fight Against Mass Tourism. Target audience: Europeans aged 25-50 interested in urban politics. Core emotion: Tension — the viewer should feel the conflict between economic benefit and community destruction. Presenter appears in the thumbnail.

### Output

**Thumbnail Brief — "Inside Barcelona's Fight Against Mass Tourism"**

**One-Frame Promise:** The viewer should feel the tension between a beautiful city and its slow destruction — something is wrong in paradise.

---

**Concept 1: The Crowd vs. The Local**
- *Emotional Hook:* Tension — the viewer feels the crushing weight of tourism on a real neighbourhood
- *Background:* A packed, narrow Barcelona street (La Rambla or Gothic Quarter) — dense with tourists, slightly motion-blurred to convey chaos and overwhelm
- *Foreground/Subject:* Presenter on the right side of frame, standing still and sharp against the blurred crowd. Expression: serious, investigative — direct eye contact with camera. Presenter is the one still point in a churning scene.
- *Text Overlay:* Left-aligned — "BARCELONA'S" (white, medium) / "BREAKING POINT" (red #E50000, large, bold). Urgent and specific.
- *Color Mood:* Warm Mediterranean tones desaturated slightly — the beauty of Barcelona is present but muted, as if something is draining the life from it
- *Production Notes:* Shoot on location or composite presenter onto a Barcelona street scene. The motion blur on the crowd is essential — it creates the visual contrast between the presenter (stable, trustworthy) and the tourist mass (chaotic, overwhelming). Avoid making the tourists look individually identifiable.

---

**Concept 2: The Protest**
- *Emotional Hook:* Conflict — something is being fought over
- *Background:* Barcelona residential balconies with protest banners — "TOURISTS GO HOME" or similar anti-tourism signage (these are real and widely photographed). Warm stone building facade.
- *Foreground/Subject:* Presenter in lower-right, looking up at the banners. Expression: thoughtful, observational — as if documenting something unfolding.
- *Text Overlay:* Upper-left — "TOURISTS" (white, large) / "NOT WELCOME" (yellow #FFD700, bold). Provocative but accurate to the movement.
- *Color Mood:* Warm sandstone (#C2B280) and Mediterranean blue sky, with the protest banners providing a jarring contrast — beauty and anger in the same frame
- *Production Notes:* If shooting on location, capture real protest banners (widely visible in Barcelona's Barceloneta and Gothic Quarter). If compositing, use documented real slogans. The presenter should look journalistic, not performative — this is documentary tone, not reaction content. Mobile-optimized: the protest text on the banners must be legible at small size.

---

**Concept 3: The Split City (Mobile-First)**
- *Emotional Hook:* Contradiction — the same city seen two ways
- *Background:* Vertical split. Left half: postcard-perfect Barcelona — blue sky, Sagrada Familia or Park Guell, vibrant colours. Right half: the same city desaturated, with a "FOR RENT" or "SE ALQUILA" sign, empty shuttered shops, a sense of abandonment.
- *Foreground/Subject:* Presenter centered on the split line, half in each world. Expression: direct, confrontational.
- *Text Overlay:* Across the bottom — "TWO BARCELONAS" (white on dark bar, bold). Clean, simple, immediate.
- *Color Mood:* Left half saturated and warm. Right half cool and grey (#808080). The contrast tells the story without reading the text.
- *Production Notes:* The split must feel like one continuous image divided by colour grading, not two separate photos. Test at mobile thumbnail size — the colour difference between halves must be obvious. This concept works without reading the text, which is ideal for mobile scroll.

**Recommended for primary publish:** Concept 1 — the crowd-vs-presenter contrast is immediately readable and communicates the documentary tone. Test Concept 3 for mobile.

**Next Step:** If shooting on location in Barcelona, capture the crowd scene and protest banners during peak tourist hours. Brief your designer with Concept 1 specifications. Run seo-title-optimizer to verify the text overlay aligns with your final title.

## Input 05: Vague/ambiguous input
**Prompt:** Video title: Life Advice. Target audience: young adults. Core emotion: Something motivational.

### Output

**Thumbnail Brief — "Life Advice"**

**One-Frame Promise:** The viewer should feel like someone trustworthy and relatable is about to tell them something they need to hear.

---

**Concept 1: The Direct Address**
- *Emotional Hook:* Trust and relatability — a real person speaking honestly
- *Background:* Soft, warm gradient — light peach (#FFDAB9) fading to warm white. Clean, uncluttered, approachable.
- *Foreground/Subject:* Presenter centered, slightly off-center right. Expression: warm, slightly serious, direct eye contact — the look of someone about to tell you something important but not lecture you. Shoulders visible, casual clothing.
- *Text Overlay:* Left-aligned, vertically centered — "LIFE ADVICE" (dark charcoal #333333, large, clean sans-serif) / "nobody tells you" (lighter grey #666666, smaller, italic). The subtext creates the curiosity gap.
- *Color Mood:* Warm neutrals — peach, cream, charcoal. Feels personal and intimate, not corporate or hype.
- *Production Notes:* Shoot with soft, diffused lighting. No harsh shadows. The warmth should come from the light itself, not a filter. The presenter's expression is the most important element — it must feel like a real conversation, not a performance.

---

**Concept 2: The List Tease**
- *Emotional Hook:* Curiosity — specific number creates expectation
- *Background:* Clean white (#FFFFFF) with a faint ruled-notebook texture — like a page from a journal
- *Foreground/Subject:* No presenter. A handwritten-style list with items partially visible — the first item readable ("1. Nobody has it figured out"), the rest fading or cut off. A pen or pencil resting on the page.
- *Text Overlay:* Top of frame — "LIFE ADVICE" (handwritten-style font, dark ink blue #1B2A4A, large). No second line needed — the visible list item does the work.
- *Color Mood:* White, ink blue (#1B2A4A), and warm paper tones — nostalgic, personal, analog
- *Production Notes:* Can photograph a real notebook page or create digitally with a script font. The first visible list item must be intriguing enough to drive clicks. Keep total visible text minimal. Mobile-optimized: the handwritten texture and first list item must be readable at small size.

---

**Concept 3: Bold Text (Mobile-First)**
- *Emotional Hook:* Urgency — the text alone is the hook
- *Background:* Solid black (#000000)
- *Foreground/Subject:* No presenter, no imagery. Pure text.
- *Text Overlay:* Centered, stacked — "LIFE" (white, massive) / "ADVICE" (warm gold #FFB347, massive, slightly larger). Two words, full frame.
- *Color Mood:* Black and gold — premium, weighty, like something worth paying attention to
- *Production Notes:* Use a bold sans-serif (e.g., Futura Heavy, Helvetica Black). The gold must be warm, not metallic or garish. Test at 120x68px — both words must be instantly legible. This is the mobile-first option and works as an A/B test against Concept 1.

**Recommended for primary publish:** Concept 1 — the human face creates trust and click-through for advice content. A/B test Concept 3 for mobile if the presenter prefers not to appear.

**Next Step:** Shoot the presenter portrait in soft, warm light. Open your design tool and build Concept 1. Run seo-title-optimizer to explore whether a more specific title ("Life Advice Nobody Tells You" or "Life Advice for Your 20s") would outperform the generic title.
