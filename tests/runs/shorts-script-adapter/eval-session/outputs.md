# Shorts Script Adapter — Eval Outputs

## Input 01: Standard use case with clear source material
**Prompt:** Source material about cooking and following recipes too literally. Core point: Stop following recipes literally — learn the principle, then improvise. Target length: 55 seconds.

### Output

**Shorts Script**
*Estimated runtime: 50 seconds*

---

[DIRECT TO CAMERA — confident, slightly conspiratorial]

The biggest mistake you are making in the kitchen? Following recipes too literally.

Professional chefs do not measure everything. They taste. They adjust. They improvise.

[beat]

A recipe is a suggestion — not a contract.

Here is the shift: once you understand the principle behind a technique — why you sear meat at high heat, why you salt pasta water, why you rest dough — you can adapt it to whatever you have in the fridge.

[lean in slightly]

That is when cooking stops being stressful and starts being creative. Stop reading recipes like instructions. Start reading them like suggestions.

---

**On-Screen Text Suggestions**

- **0:00–0:03:** "Stop doing this when you cook" [bold, white on dark]
- **0:06–0:08:** "PROFESSIONAL CHEFS DON'T MEASURE EVERYTHING" [caps, emphasis]
- **0:12–0:14:** "A recipe is a suggestion" [clean, centered]
- **0:22–0:26:** "Understand the PRINCIPLE" [keyword highlight]
- **0:38–0:42:** "Stressful → Creative" [transition graphic]

## Input 03: Minimal input
**Prompt:** Source material about drinking coffee at the wrong time. Core point: Delay your first coffee by 90 minutes. Target length: 30 seconds.

### Output

**Shorts Script**
*Estimated runtime: 28 seconds*

---

[DIRECT TO CAMERA — quick, confident]

You are drinking your coffee at the wrong time.

When you wake up, your cortisol — your body's natural alertness hormone — is already high. Drinking coffee on top of high cortisol means you are wasting it.

The fix: wait 90 minutes after waking. Let cortisol drop naturally, then hit the caffeine. You will feel it more, and you will crash less.

---

## Input 05: Vague/ambiguous input
**Prompt:** Source material: vague content about habits — people struggle, it takes time, consistency is important, start small. Core point: Something about habits. Target length: 45 seconds.

### Output

**Shorts Script**
*Estimated runtime: 42 seconds*

---

[DIRECT TO CAMERA — grounded, no hype]

The reason most habits fail is not motivation. It is scale.

People try to change everything at once. New workout routine, new diet, new morning schedule — all on the same Monday. By Wednesday, it collapses.

[beat]

The fix is almost insultingly simple: make the habit so small it feels pointless. Two minutes of stretching. One page of a book. One glass of water before coffee.

[look at camera]

You are not building the habit yet. You are building the consistency. The habit scales later. The consistency has to come first.

---

**On-Screen Text Suggestions**

- **0:00–0:03:** "Why your habits keep FAILING" [bold hook]
- **0:10–0:13:** "Everything. At once. On Monday." [stacked text, comedic timing]
- **0:20–0:24:** "Make it EMBARRASSINGLY small" [emphasis on key phrase]
- **0:34–0:38:** "Consistency first. Scale later." [closing takeaway]
